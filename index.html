Position Statement

Design starts with real problems

I’m currently studying Interaction Design at USYD, and my ultimate career goal is to become a Product Designer. I believe that before starting to design a product, the process should begin with identifying the problem. Many designs may appear to solve a problem on the surface, but they do not address users’ real needs.

I also place great importance on user testing, because without testing an idea, it is difficult to determine whether it actually works, and it is also hard to know how to adjust it in the next step.

I believe that good design should not only look attractive, but also be genuinely useful and able to solve problems that have not yet been solved.

Intent

Intent

When working on this project, I first identified the target users of this product as fans of O’Sullivan and snooker fans. The main issue I considered in this project was how to transform an abstract sports story into a commemorative product that people can understand, that can be manufactured and held in the hand, and that is visible and tangible.

Design Theory Zine

This is a design theory zine created to summarize the design theories learned in DESN1001. The content includes different design themes, related design case studies, critical analysis, and five complex transformation exercises. Through this project, I learned how to express theoretical research in a visual form, and how to understand and transform everyday objects from a design perspective.

Shopping Calendar App

This is a prototype that combines shopping and calendar functions. Users can view their shopping plans, set up automatic purchases, and complete payments within the app. Through this project, I learned that good interaction design allows users to understand what they should do at each step with very little learning effort.
